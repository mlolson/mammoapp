Introduction



Summary

Mammoapp is a game that attempts to mimic a mammogram batch reading session in your web browser. The goal is to locate and correctly identify as many "correct" findings as possible using the web viewer tools. **Note however, that the "correct" findings in the example set are for software testing purposes only, they almost certainly not correct!** The beta version allows you to "play" one test set of five mammo cases. In the future I hope to make it possible to upload your own training sets and mark your own findings.

Navigation

Two views of each mammogram are available: the user can switch between the mediolateral-oblique view (MLO) and the chest compression view (CC) by clicking the orientation boxes on the right. (Pink arrow). You can also move the window around the image by clicking and dragging in the orientation boxes.

The canvas is zoomed in and out using the mouse wheel or, alternatively, using the two buttons in the upper right corner may be user (Blue arrow).

Marking Findings

To mark a finding, you may use one of the Region of Interest (ROI) tools (Red Arrow). To make a rectangle ROI, click the "Rect ROI" button, click on the canvas, and drag. To define a polygon ROI, click the "Poly ROI" button, click on the canvas to mark your starting point, then continue marking points around the ROI. Close the ROI by clicking on your starting point.

Once you complete an ROI, you will be asked to provide information about the finding. Fill out the description and mark the type, then click "Save & Close". You may select an existing finding by clicking on its label. This will bring up the dialog box, allowing you to alter or delete it.

Entering an Impression

To finish a case, click the "Show Impression" button. This will bring up a dialog showing a list of all your findings, plus a place to mark a BIRADS category, impression, indications, and final pathology. When you are finished press "Save & Submit".

Scoring

In this beta version the software scores you only on whether you correctly located the findings.
